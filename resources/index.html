Clojurians Slack

Clojurians Slack is largest communication hub for the Clojure community.

Inside the Slack, you can

  • get help from experts in the community
  • meet others using Clojure for fun and profit
  • chat directly with project maintainers
  • hear the latest news and developments in the community

We're fun, friendly, and helpful. Come join us!

We want this to be a friendly and helpful space for everyone and the Admin team here tries to be very active as moderators, to keep things that way.

  • If you are new to Slack and need help navigating around, ask in the #slack-help channel.
  • If you encounter spam or any kind of harassment, please report it in the #spam-reports channel (or, if you do not feel comfortable posting about it in public, reach out via direct message to any of the Admins who are online -- the list of Admins can be found in the Code of Conduct document).
  • We encourage the use of threads in Slack so that back and forth discussions do not clutter up the channels, allowing multiple conversations to happen at the same time without "talking over" each other.

There are really only two rules:

  • Jobs openings may be posted only in the dedicated channels: #jobs and #remote-jobs and must be Clojure/ClojureScript jobs -- recruiters take note! You may be able to find candidates in the #available-for-jobs channel (do not post jobs there!).
  • We have a zero tolerance policy for spam and unsolicited direct messages of a commercial nature (especially about things like Upwork) -- violators will have their accounts deactivated!

As a reminder, Clojure communities try to follow these guidelines for general conduct -- Community Etiquette -- and this Slack also has a specific Code of Conduct.

Once you join, why not say "Hi!" and tell folks a bit about yourself and why you're here, in the #introduce-yourself channel!
